Road to $1k/moEpisode 3 · 26 Feb 2026

🥷🏽 Mitch Koko 💰 $19 / mo 🔥 Ritualz 💳 Expensif 💬 Tuteee 🎮 Blink Battle

Three months ago I started this road to $1k series where my app was making $8 a month. Now the revenue is $19 a month and I've released a few more apps. The goal is to make at least $1k per month from these apps and I'm currently focusing on these 4 moving forward.

I built all of them with Flutter for cross-platform, but I also write native Swift and Kotlin for things like home screen widgets. Supabase is my backend. I buy all my domains from Porkbun - simple and cheap - and each app has its own landing page.

💰 MRR $19 / mo

The revenue right now is mostly coming from Ritualz and Expensif since they've been out the longest and have the most users. But I actually think Tuteee and Blink Battle have higher potential for monetisation - they're just new so I need to get them in front of more people.

The monetisation model is something I've been thinking a lot about. I use RevenueCat for managing all the subscriptions and one-time payments across both app stores, and Google AdMob for reward ads. And the basic flow I've been testing is - you give the user the full app for free, let them use it, let them get invested. And then at a natural point they hit a wall. When they hit that wall a dialog box pops up with two options: watch a reward ad, or pay to remove that friction. That way even free users generate revenue and the ones who love the app convert to paying over time.

🎮 Blink Battle Game

In Blink Battle, you get 5 lives. Once you're out, you watch an ad for 5 more lives or buy them as a one-time purchase. This game is inspired by Starcraft which was my favourite game growing up. Took me a couple days to build and get on the app store. And I reckon this same game would've taken me months at least. Months of extremely hard work.

Bottleneck
Difficulty curve. Users can't get past level 2.
Solution
Make the game easier and more rewarding early on.

💬 Tuteee AI Math Tutor

In Tuteee, after 10 messages with the AI tutor you hit the wall. You've been learning, you're in the middle of a problem, you're invested. So you either watch an ad to keep going or subscribe for pro. This is the app I'm most excited about. I've been refining it since last month - added graph input so users can draw and plot graphs directly, made the AI smarter at tracking what the student knows.

And it's funny because right after I built that graph input, Claude released interactive charts as a feature. Which is amazing but it makes you think about moats. Like what is the actual problem I'm solving and what edge do I have. AI is getting so good so fast that maybe all of this gets replaced eventually. I don't know.

Bottleneck
Distribution. Need to get it in front of actual students.
Solution
Reach out through school groups and study communities.

🔥 Ritualz Habit Tracker

My first real app and where most of the revenue is coming from. Added group habits and a streak leaderboard this month.

Bottleneck
Organic growth. Need social features to drive a viral loop.
Solution
Market the group habits and leaderboard features.

💳 Expensif Personal Finance

Built a smart CSV import using the Claude API that handles any bank format. App is solid now.

Bottleneck
Users. Need to get it in front of more people.
Solution
Create content showing the CSV import and premium features.

What's Next

$19 a month, month 3. Growing slowly. I've been spending most of my time building and not enough time getting these apps in front of people. So next month I want to flip that. Fix the difficulty in Blink Battle, push Tuteee to actual students, and start making more content around the apps themselves.

But honestly the thing I keep coming back to is just how fast everything is moving. AI is getting better literally every day at this point. Building and releasing an app used to take me months or even years. Now I can do it in days. And it's changing what the job even is - I'm becoming more of a product person now, thinking about taste and design and what's actually worth making, because the coding part isn't really the bottleneck anymore. It's really more about solving a real problem.

I've actually never had this much fun coding and building things. I genuinely don't know where this is all going. But I can't stop using it, it's helping me so much.
